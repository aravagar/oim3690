## What I learned

- The basic skeleton of every page is the same: `<!DOCTYPE html>`, then `<html>`, then `<head>` for the metadata and `<body>` for everything you actually see.
- Semantic tags are not just for looks. Using `<header>`, `<nav>`, `<main>`, and `<footer>` instead of throwing `<div>` at everything makes the page readable for screen readers and search engines. You only reach for `<div>` when nothing semantic fits.
- Alt text matters. Every image needs a real description, not a filename.
- The READ-FIND-ASK-VERIFY workflow for reading AI code. Instead of asking AI to explain a whole file, I tell it what I already understand and ask it to explain only the parts I do not. That made the explanations way more useful.
- Command Palette in VS Code (Ctrl+Shift+P) is the fastest way to do almost anything.
